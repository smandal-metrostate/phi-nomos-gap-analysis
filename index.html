Regulatory Text

45 CFR §164.308(b)(1) — Business Associate Contracts

A covered entity may permit a business associate to create, receive, maintain, or transmit protected health information on the covered entity’s behalf only if the covered entity obtains satisfactory assurances, in the form of a written contract or other arrangement, that the business associate will appropriately safeguard the information.

45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(ii)(J) — Disposition of PHI at Termination

The contract between a covered entity and a business associate must provide that, upon termination of the contract, the business associate will return or destroy all protected health information received from, or created or received on behalf of, the covered entity, if feasible.

45 CFR §164.410 / §164.404 — Breach Notification

Following discovery of a breach of unsecured protected health information, a covered entity must notify each affected individual, and HHS, without unreasonable delay and in no case later than 60 days after discovery.

Compliance Scenario

Scenario

MedTech Solutions provides billing services to Riverside Clinic under a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) executed in 2022. During an internal audit in July 2026, Riverside discovers that MedTech’s BAA lapsed on renewal in January 2025 due to an administrative oversight — but MedTech continued to receive and transmit patient PHI for billing throughout 2025 and into 2026 without a BAA in force. The lapse was never noticed until this audit; no notifications were sent when it was discovered, and the engagement is ongoing.

Compliance Question

Is Riverside Clinic compliant with the Business Associate Agreement requirement at §164.308(b)(1) with respect to the PHI transmitted to MedTech during the lapse period — and what downstream obligations does that gap trigger?

Answer Choices

  • 1 Yes, Riverside is compliant — the original 2022 BAA covers the relationship regardless of renewal status.
  • 2 No — the lapse is a gap at §164.308(b)(1) that also activates the Breach Notification duty at §164.410.
  • 3 Need more information about whether MedTech safeguarded the PHI despite the lapse.
